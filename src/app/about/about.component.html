Ernest Hemingway: A Biography

Ernest Hemingway was an American novelist and short story writer, widely regarded as one of the greatest writers of the 20th century. Born on July 21, 1899, in Illinois, Hemingway's writing style was characterized by its concise and straightforward prose. He drew inspiration from his experiences as a journalist and war correspondent, infusing his works with themes of masculinity, adventure, and existentialism. Some of his notable works include "The Old Man and the Sea," "A Farewell to Arms," and "For Whom the Bell Tolls," which earned him the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1954. Hemingway's legacy continues to influence and inspire writers around the world.
